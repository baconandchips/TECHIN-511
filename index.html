Remote Control Holder: by Team 11

Remote Control Holder

Helping remote controls stay reachable, one table at a time

Team members: Guanting Li and Xuefei Chen

Description of Design

For our team, we focused on how people have a tendency to misplace their remote controls all over the place, leading to frustration whenever the next person sits down and cannot find the remote control anywhere in sight. With this in mind, we set out to design a holder that can stow remote controls underneath a table, focusing on both functionality and form aesthetics. Our intended users are people (like ourselves) who want to have the remote controls easily accessible to us at any given moment.

In our design, we used an S-shape curve to help guide the table edge into our design with one side of the curve, and hold a remote control with the other side of the curve. The electronic components inside our prototype allows for the remote control holder to light up whenever a remote is not placed within, thus gently reminding users of placing back the remote. This allows our users to always keep their remotes in a set location, reducing their chances of losing their remote controls constantly. There also is a modular stackable component within our design, which allows for multiple holders to connect with each other and form a larger "hub" for remote controls.

(Pictured above: modular stackable components. One part connects with the other, if two or more of this holder design is present. Parts are toleranced to appropriate measures.)
